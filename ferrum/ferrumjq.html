Fe oxidation state calculation for amphiboles

This method allows to compute Fe2+ and Fe3+ in amphiboles using stoichiometric criteria assuming that iron is the only cation with variable oxidation state and that oxygen is the only anion. Works for most calcic amphiboles, especially for hornblendes, edenites, pargasites and actinolites. Just enter the oxide percents with all iron reported as FeO and click "Calculate". To convert Fe2O3 to FeO multiple the amount by 0.8998. Fe3+ will show up in formula units if it is present. For more info see Droop (1987), eq. 6. Link to the paper.
