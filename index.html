The results of chemical analysis of minerals and rocks are most often presented in weight percent of oxides such as SiO2, Al2O3, FeO, H2O, etc. In mineralogical studies however it is more useful to present chemical composition as formulas. The same way composition of water is more pleasantly expressed as H2O, not 89 wt. % O and 11 wt. % H. The Adularia is an attempt to gather together various method of computing formulas for major rock-forming minerals. We started out with the most commonly used method, anion-based method that allows simple calculations for most rock-forming minerals. In addition, we provide a method to compute Fe2+/Fe3+ ratio in complex iron-bearing minerals such as chlorites, amphiboles and pyroxenes. So far we only implemented the calculation for amphiboles since this group of minerals is more complex yet very abundant.
